Program Overview

Kaplan Nursing has been developing testing products and preparing nursing students to pass the NCLEX-RN® for more than 30 years. In 1999, Kaplan Nursing launched the first version of assessment testing. Kaplan Nursing has been supporting nursing school faculty and their students ever since.

The Kaplan Nursing Integrated Testing Program is comprised of a series of online testing and remediation resources designed to evaluate and repair the nursing knowledge of students in an undergraduate pre-licensure program. Testing results enable nursing school faculty to identify at-risk students in real time. Early intervention changes student outcomes.

The assets provided through the Kaplan Nursing Integrated Testing Program are briefly summarized below. Your individual program may provide all or only some of these resources, depending on what your school has chosen.

Online Resources

Nursing School Success: This section of the website contains a series of videos designed to teach the basic study and self-management skills needed to be successful as a nursing student. A Dosage and Calculation workshop is also provided.

Integrated Testing: This section includes secure, proctored, nationally-normed exams designed to be used at the end of class, at the end the program, or as an exit evaluation of student performance. Complete feedback and remediation are available to both students and faculty at the completion of the exam.

Focused Review Tests: These are unsecured tests designed for formative assessment and remediation of nursing school content throughout the curriculum. Students may use these as pretests for a classroom quiz or test, as practice for the Kaplan Integrated Tests, or as post-test remediation of content weakness. Detailed rationales and in-depth content review are provided on-screen for each item during the remediation process.

Mid-Fidelity Simulations (RN only): Organized by the steps of the nursing process, these online, interactive simulations ask students to use their clinical reasoning and critical thinking skills to identify “pertinent items” at each step of the client care process.

Essential Nursing Skills: These are short videos demonstrating need-to-know clinical nursing skills.

NCLEX® Prep: This is a complete resource library containing practice questions (Qbank), Question Trainer practice tests, content and question review videos, and other valuable assets to ensure students have everything they need to be successful on Test Day.

Kaplan Nursing School Admissions Test: Designed to be used as a pre-admission assessment or as a post-entry evaluation for identification of at-risk students, this test is comprised of reading, writing, math, and physio-based science. Please note: The Nursing Admissions Test is administered through a separate website. If you would like access to this test, please contact your Account Manager for sign-in information and an orientation. You can find your Account Manager’s contact information on the Home page.

Books

Students enrolled in the Kaplan Nursing Integrated Testing program have access to two texts. All students are given access to eBooks; schools may elect to purchase hard/paper copies as well.

  1. The Basics Book: A high-yield content review designed to cover need-to-know concepts in an easy-to-understand outline form, replete with summary charts and clear illustrations. This book can be used as a study guide and as a remediation tool for basic nursing content.
  2. The Kaplan NCLEX® Content Review Guide: A comprehensive NCLEX® resource containing a study plan, test-taking strategies, and nursing content sorted by Client Needs Categories to match the NCLEX® test plan.

ADA Testing Instructions

Students who are designated as needing accommodations during testing sessions will receive untimed versions of their tests. To request extended test timing, please email the name of the student to your Kaplan Account Manager. Your Account Manager will mark the student in the Kaplan system as having ADA status.

When students are set to ADA status, once their Integrated test times down to 0, a pop-up window will appear telling them they can continue (see screenshot below). After students click the “OK” button in the pop-up, their test will become untimed. It is up to the proctor to ensure that ADA students receive the correct amount of time for which they are eligible. Therefore, the proctor will need to be actively monitoring the timing for those students and aware of what their official accommodations are.

Please note that ADA status affects all Integrated Tests for such students.

To request other ADA accommodations outside of test timing, you and/or the students can contact our Accessibility Specialist directly at special.services@kaplan.com to discuss available accommodations for Kaplan testing and resources. The students may be asked to complete an application form (available in print or e-reader-compatible electronic format) so we can better understand their specific needs. In the event a student cannot complete the application, our Accessibility Specialist can gather the needed information from the student by phone or email.

Purpose and Test Design

Integrated Tests benchmark student progress during nursing school. The test items cover the key concepts and major topics taught in a basic nursing curriculum. The tests assess the ability of students to use the components of the nursing process within major content and conceptual areas. The tests strategically include questions of varying difficulty levels to assess students’ ability to apply the nursing process to client needs, problems, and frequently encountered situations. Constructs, such as critical thinking and making nursing judgments, are essential themes embedded in all tests. The tests are designed to challenge basic nursing students and provide feedback regarding students’ ability to recall information, understand concepts, set priorities, and make nursing judgments.

The Integrated Tests are developed by groups of nursing faculty selected by Kaplan Nursing. After development, each Integrated Test is reviewed by a panel of nursing faculty to determine the degree to which the items on the test reflect a course’s content domain. The panel makes changes to the Integrated Test as needed to make the tests reflective of a basic nursing curriculum leading to licensure.

To ensure the usefulness and effectiveness of the tests as evaluation tools, Kaplan Nursing conducts research studies to determine the reliability and validity of the tests. In these studies, Kaplan Nursing administers the RN Integrated Tests to students enrolled in associate degree (ADN) and baccalaureate degree (BSN) nursing programs and administers the PN Integrated Tests to groups of practical nursing and vocational nursing students. A small number of students enrolled in nursing diploma programs participate in the RN study. The schools of nursing participating in the study are geographically dispersed across the United States. Kaplan Nursing makes every effort to ensure that the sample that participates in the study accurately represents the population of students enrolled in nursing schools.

The Integrated Tests are administered by the faculty of the nursing schools under proctored conditions. Nursing faculty monitor the security of the test questions. Because the tests are designed to be used for evaluation rather than as teaching tools, traditional explanations of the correct and incorrect answers for the Integrated Tests are not distributed to students or faculty. Instead, remediation explanations are provided for each test question.

Repeat analysis of data from the Integrated Tests has shown that results are similar to the results from the previous studies. This suggests that the Integrated Tests are stable and reliable instruments. The Integrated Tests continue to be monitored for quality and updated as needed.

The Integrated Tests are intended to assist faculty and administrators in ascertaining students’ strengths and weaknesses in recalling and understanding nursing knowledge and in applying this knowledge in clinical situations. In addition, a significant number of questions require students to apply critical thinking skills to make sound nursing judgments within the purview of a basic nursing course or program. Test results enable nursing school faculty at the end of the program to identify a student's knowledge and learning deficits in specific content areas.

In accordance with the industry's Fair Testing standards, Kaplan Nursing recommends that the Integrated Tests not be used as the sole criteria for progression in or graduation from a nursing program, but considered one of several indicators of students’ accomplishments in basic nursing.

  • List of PN Integrated Tests By Percentile

Integrated tests may contain any of the following type of items:

  1. Four-option, multiple-choice questions
  2. Multiple-response questions (select all that apply)
  3. Fill-in-the-blank questions (math/numeric entry)
  4. Hot-spot questions
  5. Ordered-response questions

The Integrated Tests question interface is similar to the test interface used on the NCLEX-RN® and PN exams. Students see a question stem with answer choices, a pop-up calculator, and a countdown clock. Students make forward progress through the test and are not able to return to questions they have already answered. Students click the radio button next to their selected answer choice. To move to the next question, students select the Next tab. To finish the test, students select the Quit tab.

Overview and Purpose

The Focused Review Tests are unproctored practice tests that students access at any time during their enrollment in the Integrated Testing Program. These tests review basic nursing content according to the medical model and provide topical retesting opportunities. The tests are designed to coach students through the specific content, providing rationales for the correct and incorrect answers, comprehensive content remediation, and alternative question stems to enhance student comprehension of written questions.

The Focused Review Tests may be accessed on the student home page from any computer with Internet access. All RN Focused Review Tests contain 30 questions. PN Focused Review tests contain 15 questions. All math calculation tests for RN and PN contain 10 questions.

Mid-Fidelity Client Simulations (RN only)

Completing mid-fidelity simulations requires students to critically think and make clinical judgments. The Kaplan Integrated Testing Program includes six client simulations divided into modules. These modules are organized according to the steps of the nursing process and are designed to help students improve their critical thinking skills. The cases include the following:

  • Fundamentals: Nursing
  • Adult Health: Type 2 Diabetes
  • Pediatric Nursing: Epilepsy
  • Obstetrical Nursing: Premature Rupture of Membranes
  • Adult Health: Psychiatric Nursing
  • Adult Health: COPD

Kaplan Nursing recognizes that there are certain behaviors required for making appropriate clinical judgments. Students need to engage in the following behaviors while completing the simulations:

  • Identify fact versus assumption
  • Validate information
  • Identify missing information
  • Cluster related data
  • Identify patterns
  • Draw valid conclusions
  • Set priorities
  • Evaluate and/or revise thinking

In each case study, students are presented with a description of a client situation including a medical diagnosis. Students progress through care of the client using the modules organized by the nursing process framework. This makes it easy for students to identify strengths and weaknesses in prioritization and case management. Links to online learning resources are provided for additional study. The modules for each case study include the following:

  • Assessment: Identification and linking of pertinent client data to the appropriate categories; the client data presented in chart format include lab values, x-rays, and diagnostic tests
  • Physical Exam: Completion of a virtual physical examination, including selection of appropriate virtual equipment and evaluation of realistic heart and lung sounds plus determination of priority nursing diagnoses for the client based on the available client data from the chart and physical examination
  • Care planning/implementation
  • Evaluation: Evaluation of client’s outcomes based on data supplied in the situation and chart information

Integrated Testing, Focused Review, and NCLEX® Reports

As a faculty member, you have access to multiple reports through your Kaplan website that provide you with a wealth of information on your students’ performance.

The Student Report Card contains most of the basic information regarding individual student performance and also provides you with a way to monitor use of online remediation resources.

The Remediation Time by Question report, which you can access by clicking on a student’s remediation time within the student report card, will show you that student’s remediation time in seconds for each item. The screenshot below shows an Integrated Test Report.

To see a specific student’s performance report, click on the test name. This link will take you to the student’s results by category (Student Performance by Test). The percentile rank (on Integrated Tests) is positioned above the overall or raw percent correct on the screen. The number correct, the number incorrect, and the number not reached are noted. If there are questions the student didn’t reach, it is because the student ran out of time or quit the test before completing it.

Answer-changing behavior is detailed on the right mid-screen.

The student’s performance according to level of difficulty, nursing process, clinical concept, demographic, and client need category is provided in easy-to-understand bar graphs. The check mark indicates the number of questions that were answered correctly. The blue line indicates the number of questions that were omitted. “N” indicates the total number of questions for each category.

Please note: You can select each student individually or select all students to review an entire cohort or multiple cohorts.

To view Test Results by Question, select the cohort and group (if applicable) from which you would like to view results, using the dropdown menus in the center of your screen.

You will see one test at a time on this report. You will be able to compare the national results (on Integrated tests) by topic to your cohort’s results. Focused review tests will provide you with the cohort’s performance on the topic title. The percentage provided is the p-value for each question (the percentage of students who answered the question correctly).
