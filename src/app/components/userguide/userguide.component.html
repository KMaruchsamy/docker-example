PROGRAM OVERVIEW

The Kaplan Nursing Integrated Testing Program consists of a series of online testing and remediation resources designed to evaluate and remediate the nursing knowledge of students in an undergraduate pre-licensure program. Testing results enable nursing school faculty to:

  • identify at -risk students in real time
  • evaluate course outcomes to identify gaps
  • assess and evaluate program outcomes in relation to accreditation, QSEN, and client need categories (among many others)

ADA TESTING INSTRUCTIONS

Students who are designated as needing accommodations during testing sessions will receive untimed versions of their tests. To request extended test timing, please click "Manage Cohorts" from the main menu of the Faculty Site. Select the cohort these students are in and select "Request Change or Update". Check the box next to the students you want that status activated on and click "Review summary of changes/updates". That will submit the request to your Account Manager and will mark the student in the Kaplan system as having ADA status. When students are set to ADA status, once their Integrated test times down to 0, a pop-up window will appear telling them they can continue. After students click the “OK” button in the pop-up, their test will become untimed. It is up to the proctor to ensure that ADA students receive the correct amount of time for which they are eligible. Therefore, the proctor will need to be actively monitoring the timing for those students and aware of what their official accommodations are.

Please note that ADA status affects all future Integrated Tests for marked students. The designation only has to be made once. There is no need to designate again for future tests

IINTEGRATE TESTING (SECURE)

PURPOSE AND TEST DESIGN

Integrated Tests benchmark student progress during nursing school. The test items cover the key concepts and major topics taught in a basic nursing curriculum. The tests assess the ability of students to use the components of the nursing process within major content and conceptual areas. The tests strategically include questions of varying difficulty levels to assess students’ ability to apply the nursing process to client needs, problems, and frequently encountered situations. Constructs, such as critical thinking and making nursing judgments, are essential themes embedded in all tests. The tests are designed to challenge basic nursing students and provide feedback regarding students’ ability to recall information, understand concepts, set priorities, and make nursing judgments.

The Integrated Tests are developed by groups of nursing faculty selected by Kaplan Nursing. After development, each Integrated Test is reviewed by a panel of nursing faculty to determine the degree to which the items on the test reflect a course’s content domain. The panel makes changes to the Integrated Test as needed to make the tests reflective of a basic nursing curriculum leading to licensure.

To ensure the usefulness and effectiveness of the tests as evaluation tools, Kaplan Nursing conducts research studies to determine the reliability and validity of the tests. In these studies, Kaplan Nursing administers the RN Integrated Tests to students enrolled in associate degree (ADN) and baccalaureate degree (BSN) nursing programs and administers the PN Integrated Tests to groups of practical nursing and vocational nursing students. A small number of students enrolled in nursing diploma programs participate in the RN study. The schools of nursing participating in the study are geographically dispersed across the United States. Kaplan Nursing makes every effort to ensure that the sample that participates in the study accurately represents the population of students enrolled in nursing schools.

The Integrated Tests are administered by the faculty of the nursing schools under proctored conditions. Nursing faculty monitor the security of the test questions. Because the tests are designed to be used for evaluation rather than as teaching tools, traditional explanations of the correct and incorrect answers for the Integrated Tests are not distributed to students or faculty. Instead, remediation explanations are provided for each test question.

Repeat analysis of data from the Integrated Tests has shown that results are similar to the results from the previous studies. This suggests that the Integrated Tests are stable and reliable instruments. The Integrated Tests continue to be monitored for quality and updated as needed.

The Integrated Tests are intended to assist faculty and administrators in ascertaining students’ strengths and weaknesses in recalling and understanding nursing knowledge and in applying this knowledge in clinical situations. In addition, a significant number of questions require students to apply critical thinking skills to make sound nursing judgments within the purview of a basic nursing course or program. Test results enable nursing school faculty at the end of the program to identify a student's knowledge and learning deficits in specific content areas.

In accordance with the industry's Fair Testing standards, Kaplan Nursing recommends that the Integrated Tests not be used as the sole criteria for progression in or graduation from a nursing program, but considered one of several indicators of students’ accomplishments in basic nursing.

Integrated tests may contain any of the following type of items:

  1. Four-option, multiple-choice questions
  2. Multiple-response questions (select all that apply)
  3. Fill-in-the-blank questions (math/numeric entry)
  4. Hot-spot questions

The Integrated Tests question interface is similar to the test interface used on the NCLEX-RN® and PN exams. Students see a question stem with answer choices, a pop-up calculator, and a countdown clock. Students make forward progress through the test and are not able to return to questions they have already answered. Students click the radio button next to their selected answer choice. To move to the next question, students select the Next tab. To finish the test, students select the Quit tab.

CUSTOMIZATION OF TESTS

IF a Kaplan standard end-of-course test does not match the curriculum of a nursing school, Kaplan Nursing can customize one end-of-course test to map to the individual course syllabus. To arrange for the development of a customized test, please contact your Nurse Consultant Kaplan Nursing requires a minimum of 12 weeks to complete the development of a customized test. One customized end-of-course test will be developed without an additional charge. It is highly recommended for faculty to review the test (available in student mode only) before administering to students to ensure a satisfactory match. Any revisions can then be made before students take the test.If revisions are needed on existing custom tests, notify your Nurse Consultant.

Please note: Custom tests will not have norming data (no percentile ranks), however many individual questions will have data (p-values) on the Test Result by Question report. All custom tests are to be given in a secure proctored setting.

FOCUSED REVIEW TESTS (UNSECURE)

Overview and purpose

The Focused Review Tests are unproctored practice tests that students access at any time during their enrollment in the Integrated Testing Program. These tests review basic nursing content according to the medical model and provide topical retesting opportunities. The tests are designed to coach students through the specific content, providing rationales for the correct and incorrect answers, comprehensive content remediation, and alternative question stems to enhance student comprehension of written questions.

The alternative wording of the question stem is called “Plain English for Nursing.” Kaplan partnered with a linguistic modification expert to reword all focused review tests to reduce unnecessary language complexity in our questions. This alternate form of the stem is available in the remediation phase after taking the test. To read more please see, White Paper on Linguistic Modification.

Overview and purpose

  • Unsecured
  • Can be given unproctored
  • Available 24/7
  • Do not have to be scheduled
  • Can be repeated as many times as wanted
  • Remediation contains q&a, rationales for correct answer and distractors, content remediation, and alternate wording of question
  • RN = 30 questions; PN/VN = 15 questions; math = 10 questions
  • Students are allowed 72 seconds per question. If time runs out, students will receive a pop up window and can choose to select more time (because these are practice tests they are not automatically submitted).
  • May be accessed on the student home page from any computer with Internet access.

CREATE YOUR OWN TEST

Students may also create their own Focused Review tests by choosing from over 900 nursing school topics. After completing these tests, students receive rationales for the correct and incorrect answers and comprehensive content remediation in the form of a brief overview; nursing focus and concepts; and interdisciplinary focus and medical management. Most topics also include video remediation.

MID-FIDELITY CLIENT SIMULATIONS(RN ONLY)

Completing mid-fidelity simulations requires students to critically think and make clinical judgments. The Kaplan Integrated Testing Program includes six client simulations divided into modules. These modules are organized according to the steps of the nursing process and are designed to help students improve their critical thinking skills. The cases include the following:

  • Fundamentals: Nursing
  • Adult Health: Type 2 Diabetes
  • Pediatric Nursing: Epilepsy
  • Obstetrical Nursing: Premature Rupture of Membranes
  • Adult Health: Psychiatric Nursing
  • Adult Health: COPD

Kaplan Nursing recognizes that there are certain behaviors required for making appropriate clinical judgments. Students need to engage in the following behaviors while completing the simulations:

  • Identify fact versus assumption
  • Validate information
  • Identify missing information
  • Cluster related data
  • Identify patterns
  • Draw valid conclusions
  • Set priorities
  • Evaluate and/or revise thinking

In each simulation, students are presented with a description of a client situation including a medical diagnosis. Students progress through care of the client using the modules organized by the nursing process framework. This makes it easy for students to identify strengths and weaknesses in prioritization and case management. Links to online learning resources are provided for additional study. The modules for each case study include the following:

  • Assessment: Identification and linking of pertinent client data to the appropriate categories; the client data presented in chart format include lab values, x-rays, and diagnostic tests
  • Physical Exam: Completion of a virtual physical examination, including selection of appropriate virtual equipment and evaluation of realistic heart and lung sounds plus determination of priority nursing diagnoses for the client based on the available client data from the chart and physical examination
  • Care planning/implementation
  • Evaluation: Evaluation of client’s outcomes based on data supplied in the situation and chart information

At the completion of each module, students receive immediate online feedback about how they performed according to the behaviors of critical thinking. Faculty have real-time online access to students’ results and a downloadable report of individual or aggregate results. Students may repeat a simulation or a specific module. However, the first grade is the only grade that will be saved in the system and reported on the faculty site. If students repeat a module and/or simulation, they must be instructed to print a copy of their new grade as it will not be saved in the system.

NURSING SCHOOL SUCCESS TUTORIALS

This is a collection of short videos for use at the beginning of a nursing program or throughout as needed. The videos review basic study skills, time management, stress management, and nursing dosage and calculation.

The Nursing School Success Study Skills Videos series consists of the following:

  • Classes: Learn how to make the most of attending classes using techniques such as active listening.
  • Taking Notes: Find out how to take notes effectively.
  • Studying: Learn how to study in the manner that works best for you.
  • Remembering: Find out what you can do to improve your retention of information.
  • Time Management: Learn how to make the best use of your time.
  • Stress Reduction: Learn about ways you can work through stressful situations.
  • Preparing for the Exam: Learn ways to help you get ready for success on exams.

The Dosage and Calculation Workshop contains video and audio instruction. It consists of three modules, each of which is 40 to 50 minutes long. It includes review of different methods of problem solving: Basic Math, Dimensional Analysis, and Ratio and Proportion.

THE CHANNEL

This resource will provide RN and PN nursing students with classroom lectures, activities, and test items to support their current learning. It will provide students with the salient content review they’ve requested from us. It is recommended that students use the Channel resources at multiple times during their nursing education.
