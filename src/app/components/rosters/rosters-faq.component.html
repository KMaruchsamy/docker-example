FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What are cohort rosters?

All students enrolled in the Kaplan program are activated by the submission of rosters to your school’s dedicated Account Manager , {{ accountManagerFirstName }} {{ accountManagerLastName }} ( {{ accountManagerEmail }}) . The rosters tell us which students to activate and classify them into cohorts labeled by month and year of graduation (e.g. June 2017). Based on their cohort, students are granted access to the Kaplan program for three months after graduation, which typically covers their NCLEX® testing cycle.

How do I submit a new cohort roster?

To submit a new student roster please download our New Class Roster Sheet, fill it out, and then submit it here at least 10 business days prior to the requested access start date. Kaplan will activate the student accounts based on the information in the Class Roster Form within 10 business days of receipt of the form. Students will not be able to access program materials until after the roster is received and processed by Kaplan.

Depending on your contract, your school may use a specific roster form that is different from the standard Class Roster Form. If a separate form has been provided to you by your Account Manager, please continue to use that form.

What happens after I submit a cohort roster?

As students are entered into the Kaplan system, they are emailed a user name and password that provides them with access to all the resources they will utilize in conjunction with the Kaplan program.

How do I submit a change or update?

To submit a request for a roster change or update, click on the “Request change or update” link within the appropriate cohort roster. You can ask your Account Manager to make the following changes or updates:

  • Move a student to a different cohort or move a student from a different cohort to the current cohort (for example, if the student’s graduation date has changed).
  • Make a student in the current cohort inactive (for example, if the student has withdrawn for the semester or left the school).
  • Add a new student to the current cohort to enable access to Kaplan resources.
  • Request untimed versions of Kaplan tests for students in the cohort needing testing accommodations. When a student is set to ADA status, his/her tests will be untimed. Before beginning a test, the student should see an "UNTIMED" stamp next to scheduled Integrated Tests, Focused Review, and NCLEX prep resources. If the student sees a "TIMED" stamp, he/she should NOT begin the test and alert the proctor that he/she is not receiving extended time.
    • Once the test starts, the student should NOT see a timer.
    • If the student sees a timer when beginning the exam, the student should end the test immediately because he/she is not receiving extended timing.
    • Since ADA status makes tests untimed, it is up to the proctor to ensure the student receives the correct amount of time based on their specific accommodations.
    • The proctor should actively monitor the timing for each student and be aware of their official accommodations.
    • *Please note that ADA status affects all future Integrated Tests for marked students. The designation only has to be made once. There is no need to designate again for future tests.
  • If a student needs additional time to access the Kaplan resources beyond their end date (three months after graduation), students can purchase additional increments of time by contacting our Student Support Team at 877-572-8457. Students who are not successful on their first attempt at the NCLEX® can apply for the Partnership Extension and, if eligible, receive an additional three months of access at no additional cost.

Please note: Cohort rosters will not immediately reflect requested changes or updates submitted through this process. Requests for changes or updates will be submitted to your Account Manager, who will process them within three (3) business days and inform you via email when your changes have been processed. For urgent requests, please contact Faculty Support at 1-866-920-6311.
