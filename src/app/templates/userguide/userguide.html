Program Overview

Kaplan Nursing has been developing testing products and preparing nursing students to pass the NCLEX-RN® for more than 30 years. In 1999, Kaplan Nursing launched the first version of assessment testing and has been supporting nursing school faculty and their students ever since.

The Kaplan Nursing Integrated Testing Program is comprised of a series of online testing and remediation resources designed to evaluate and repair the nursing knowledge of students in an undergraduate pre-licensure program. Testing results enable nursing school faculty to identify at-risk students in real time. Early intervention changes student outcomes.

The assets provided through the Kaplan Nursing Integrated Testing program are briefly summarized below. Your individual program may provide all or only some of these resources depending on what your school has chosen.

Online Resources

Nursing School Success: This section of the website contains a series of videos designed to teach the basic study and self-management skills needed to be successful as a nursing student. A Dosage and Calculation workshop is also provided.

Integrated Testing: Secure, proctored, nationally-normed exams designed to be used at end of class, end of program, or as an exit evaluation of student performance. Complete feedback and remediation are available to both students and faculty at the completion of the exam.

Focused Review Tests: Unsecured tests designed for formative assessment and remediation of nursing school content throughout the curriculum. Students may use as a pre-test for a classroom quiz or test, as practice for the Kaplan Integrated Tests, or as post-test remediation of content weakness. Detailed rationales and in-depth content review are provided on-screen for each item during the remediation process.

Mid-Fidelity Simulations (RN only): Organized by the steps of the nursing process, these online, interactive simulations ask students to use their clinical reasoning and critical thinking skills to identify “pertinent items” at each step of the client care process.

Essential Nursing Skills: Short videos demonstrating need-to-know clinical nursing skills.

NCLEX® Prep: Complete resource library containing practice questions (Qbank), Question Trainer practice tests, content and question review videos, and other valuable assets to ensure students have everything they need to be successful on Test Day.

Kaplan Nursing School Admissions Test: Designed to be used as a pre-admission assessment or as a post-entry evaluation for identification of at-risk students, this test is comprised of reading, writing, math, and physio-based science. Please note: The Nursing Admissions Test is administered through a separate website. If you would like access to this test, please contact your Account Manager for sign-in information and an orientation. You can find your Account Manager’s contact information on the Home page.

Books

Students enrolled in the Kaplan Nursing Integrated Testing program have access to two texts. All students are given access to eBooks; schools may elect to purchase hard/paper copies as well.

  1. The Basics Book A high-yield content review designed to cover need-to know concepts in an easy-to-understand outline form, replete with summary charts and clear illustrations. This book can be used as a study guide and as a remediation tool for basic nursing content.
  2. The Kaplan NCLEX® Content Review Guide: A comprehensive NCLEX® resource containing a study plan, test taking strategies, and nursing content sorted by Client Needs Categories to match the NCLEX® test plan.

ADA Testing Instructions

Students who are designated as needing accommodations during testing sessions will receive untimed versions of their tests. To request extended test timing, please email the name of the student to your Kaplan Account Manager. Your Account Manager will mark the student in the Kaplan system as having ADA status.

When a student is set to ADA status, once their Integrated test times down to 0, a pop-up window will appear telling the student they can continue (see screenshot below). After the student clicks the “OK” button in the pop-up, their test will become untimed. It is up to the proctor to ensure the student receives the correct amount of time for which he or she is eligible. Therefore, the proctor will need to be actively monitoring the timing for that student and aware of what his or her official accommodations are.

Please note that ADA status affects all Integrated Tests for that student.

To request other ADA accommodations outside of test timing, you and/or the student can contact our Accessibility Specialist directly at special.services@kaplan.com to discuss available accommodations for Kaplan testing and resources. The student may be asked to complete an application form (available in print or e-reader compatible electronic format) so we can better understand his or her specific needs. In the event a student cannot complete the application, our Accessibility Specialist can gather the needed information by phone or email.

Overview and Purpose

The Integrated Tests are developed by groups of nursing faculty selected by Kaplan Nursing. The purpose of the tests is to evaluate knowledge of key concepts and major topics taught in a basic nursing curriculum. Constructs, such as critical thinking and making nursing judgments, are essential themes embedded in all tests. After development, each Integrated Test is reviewed by a panel of nursing faculty to determine the degree to which the items on the test reflect a course’s content domain. Changes are made as needed to make the tests reflective of a basic nursing curriculum leading to licensure.

To ensure the usefulness and effectiveness of the tests as evaluation tools, Kaplan Nursing conducts research studies to determine the reliability and validity of the tests. The Integrated Tests are administered to groups of students enrolled in associate degree (ADN) and baccalaureate degree (BSN) nursing programs for the RN Integrated Tests and to groups of practical nursing and vocational nursing students for the PN Integrated Tests. A small number of students enrolled in nursing diploma programs participate in the RN study. The schools of nursing participating in the study are geographically dispersed across the United States. Every effort is made to ensure that the sample that participates in the study accurately represents the population of students enrolled in nursing schools.

The Integrated Tests are administered by the faculty of the nursing schools under proctored conditions. Nursing faculty monitor the security of the test questions. Because the tests are designed to be used for evaluation rather than as teaching tools, traditional explanations of the correct and incorrect answers for the Integrated Tests are not distributed to students or faculty. Instead, remediation explanations are provided for each test question.

Repeat analysis of data from the Integrated Tests has shown that results are similar to the results from the previous studies. This suggests that the Integrated Tests are stable and reliable instruments. The Integrated Tests continue to be monitored for quality and updated as needed.

Test Design

Integrated Tests are comprised of a series of end-of-course specialty tests and end-of-program or exit tests. These tests benchmark student progress during nursing school. The test items cover the key concepts and major topics taught in a basic nursing curriculum. The tests assess the ability of students to use the components of the nursing process within major content and conceptual areas. The tests strategically include questions of varying difficulty levels to assess students’ ability to apply the nursing process to client needs, problems, and frequently encountered situations. The tests are designed to challenge basic nursing students and provide feedback regarding students’ ability to recall information, understand concepts, set priorities, and make nursing judgments.

Integrated tests may contain any of the following type of items:

  1. four-option, multiple choice
  2. multiple response (select-all-that apply)
  3. fill in the blank (math/numeric entry)
  4. hot spot
  5. ordered response

The Integrated Tests question interface is similar to the test interface used on the NCLEX-RN® and PN exams. Students see a question stem with answer choices, a pop-up calculator, and a countdown clock. Students progress forward through the test and are not able to return to questions they already answered. Students click the radio button next to their selected answer choice. To move to the next question, students select the Next tab. To finish the test, students select the Quit tab.

Customization of Tests

If the standard specialty end-of-course tests do not match the curriculum of your nursing school course, one end-of-course test can be customized to map to your individual course syllabus. To arrange for development of a customized test, please contact your nurse consultant.

A minimum of 12 weeks is needed to complete development of a customized test. Only one customized end-of-course test per course will be developed without additional charge. Faculty are requested to review custom tests prior to administering to students. Revisions to custom tests may be discussed/requested via an email to your nurse consultant. Custom tests will have no norming data because they are created for your own curriculum.

Overview and Purpose

The Focused Review Tests are unproctored practice tests that students access at any time during their enrollment in the Integrated Testing Program. These tests review basic nursing content according to the medical model and provide topical retest opportunities. The tests are designed to coach students through the specific content, providing rationales for the correct and incorrect answers, comprehensive content remediation as well as alternative question stems to enhance student comprehension of written questions.

The Focused Review Tests may be accessed on the student homepage from any computer with Internet access. All RN Focused Review Tests contain 30 questions. PN Focused Review tests contain 15 questions. All math calculation tests for RN and PN contain 10 questions.

Mid-Fidelity Client Simulations ( RN only)

Completing mid-fidelity simulations requires students to critically think and make clinical judgments. The Kaplan Integrated Testing Program includes six client simulations divided into modules. These modules are organized according to the steps of the nursing process, are designed to help students improve their critical thinking skills. The cases include:

  • Fundamentals: Nursing
  • Adult Health: Type 2 Diabetes
  • Pediatric Nursing: Epilepsy
  • Obstetrical Nursing: Premature Rupture Of Membranes
  • Adult Health: Psychiatric Nursing
  • Adult Health: COPD

Kaplan Nursing recognizes that there are certain behaviors required for making appropriate clinical judgments. Students need to engage in the following behaviors while completing the simulations:

  • Identify fact versus assumption
  • Validate information
  • Identify missing information
  • Cluster related data
  • Identify patterns
  • Draw valid conclusions
  • Set priorities
  • Evaluate and/or revise thinking

In each case study, students are presented with a description of a client situation including a medical diagnosis. Students progress through care of the client using the modules organized by the nursing process framework. This makes it easy for students to identify strengths and weaknesses in prioritization and case management. Links to online learning resources are provided for additional study. The modules for each case study include:

  • Assessment—identification and linking of pertinent client data to the appropriate categories; the client data presented in chart format include lab values, x-rays, and diagnostic tests
  • Physical exam—completion of a virtual physical examination including selection of appropriate virtual equipment and evaluation of realistic heart and lung sounds diagnosis/determination of priority nursing diagnoses for the client based on the available client data from the chart and physical examination
  • Care planning/implementation—develop
  • Evaluation—evaluation of client’s outcomes based on data supplied in the situation and chart information

At the completion of each module, students receive immediate online feedback about how they performed according to the behaviors of critical thinking. Faculty have real time online access to students’ results and a downloadable report of individual or aggregate results. Students may repeat a simulation or a specific module. However, the first grade is the only grade the will be saved in the system and reported on the faculty site. If a student repeats a module and/or simulation, they must be instructed to print a copy of their new grade as it will not be saved in the system.

NCLEX® Preparation

The NCLEX® Preparation Resources provide comprehensive preparation for the NCLEX-RN® and NCLEX-PN® exams. Resources include thousands of practice questions that emulate the ones seen on the actual exams. Strategy and content review videos are available online, and a four-day (RN) or two-day (PN/LVN) critical thinking NCLEX® preparation class is available online or in person.

Summary of Resources

NCLEX® Review Course: A live 4-day review for RN or 2-day for PN/LVN, held in the classroom or online and taught by a specially-trained Kaplan nurse educator, covering the specific strategies and critical thinking needed to address passing level questions on the NCLEX® examination.

NCLEX® Review Resources: A suite of tests: Qbank, Question Trainers (RN only), Sample NCLEX® Tests, NCLEX® Predictor Tests, as well as workshops, Video Content Review, Video Question(lesson) Review, and classroom posters for reference.

Integrated Testing, Focused Review, and NCLEX® Reports

As a faculty member, you have access to multiple reports through your Kaplan website that provide you with a wealth of information on your students’ performance.

The Student Report Card contains most of the basic information regarding individual student performance and also provides you with a way to monitor use of online remediation resources.

The Remediation Time by Question report, accessible by clicking on a student’s remediation time within the student report card, will show you that student’s remediation time in seconds for each item. The screenshot below shows an Integrated Test Report.

To see a specific student’s performance report, click on the test name. This link will take you to the student’s results by category (Student Performance by Test). The percentile rank (on Integrated Tests) is positioned above the overall or raw percent correct on the screen. The number correct, number incorrect, and the number not reached are noted. If there are questions the student didn’t reach, it is because the student ran out of time or quit the test before completing it.

Answer changing behavior is detailed on the right mid screen.

The student’s performance according to level of difficulty, nursing process, clinical concept, demographic, and client need category is provided in easy-to-understand bar graphs. The checkmark indicates the number of questions that were answered correctly. The blue line indicates the number of questions that were omitted. N indicates the total number of questions for each category.

Please note: You can select each student individually or select all students to review an entire cohort or multiple cohorts.

To view Test Results by Question, select the cohort and group (if applicable) from which you would like to view results, using the dropdown menus in the center of your screen.

You will see one test at a time on this report. You will be able to compare the national results (on Integrated tests) by topic to your cohort’s results. Focused review tests will provide you with the cohort’s performance on the topic title. The percentage provided is the p-value for each question (the percentage of students who answered the question correctly).
